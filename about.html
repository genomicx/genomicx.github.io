Genomic science should be open to everyone

GenomicX exists to lower the barriers to modern bioinformatics — making powerful analysis tools free, private, and as easy to use as opening a web page.

Our Mission

Bioinformatics has a barrier-to-entry problem. Most tools require command-line experience, specific operating systems, complex dependency chains, or institutional computing resources. That leaves out students learning on personal laptops, clinicians who need quick answers, researchers at under-resourced institutions, and curious minds everywhere.

GenomicX is building infrastructure to change that. We compile established, peer-reviewed bioinformatics tools into WebAssembly so they run at near-native speed, entirely in the browser. No installs. No servers. No data leaving your machine. Just open a URL and analyse.

The browser is the most universal computing platform on earth. We think it should also be the most accessible bioinformatics platform.

Principles

1

Privacy is non-negotiable

Genomic data is sensitive. Every GenomicX tool processes data locally in the browser. Nothing is uploaded, stored, or transmitted. Your data stays on your machine, always.

2

Open source, open science

Every tool is free and open source. The code is public. The methods are transparent. Science built on GenomicX tools is reproducible by anyone.

3

Accessibility over complexity

If a tool requires a manual to get started, we haven't finished building it. Good bioinformatics software should be usable by a motivated student on day one.

4

Community-driven development

GenomicX is shaped by the people who use it. Feature requests, bug reports, new tools, documentation improvements — every contribution matters.

5

Build on what works

We don't reinvent algorithms. We take proven, peer-reviewed bioinformatics tools and bring them to the browser using WebAssembly. Trusted science, new delivery.

Who Is GenomicX For?

Researchers

Run quick analyses without provisioning servers or installing dependencies. Get results in your browser, export for publications.

Students

Learn bioinformatics without battling conda environments. Hands-on with real tools, from any computer, on day one.

Clinicians

Screen genomic data without sending it to external servers. Privacy-compliant analysis at the point of need.

Citizen Scientists

Explore microbial genomics with professional-grade tools. No institutional access required.

How to Contribute

GenomicX is a community effort. Whether you write code, test tools, write documentation, or share the project with others, you're helping make bioinformatics more accessible.

Report bugs or request features. Open an issue on the relevant tool's GitHub repository. Every report helps.
Contribute code. Fork a repo, make your changes, and open a pull request. We welcome improvements of all sizes.
Port a tool to WebAssembly. Have a favourite command-line bioinformatics tool? Help us bring it to the browser.
Improve documentation. Clear docs lower the barrier to entry. Help us write tutorials, guides, or improve existing docs.
Spread the word. Share GenomicX with your lab, your students, or on social media. Visibility helps the project grow.

About the Founder

GenomicX was started by Nabil-Fareed Alikhan, a bioinformatics researcher at the Centre for Genomic Pathogen Surveillance, University of Oxford. His work focuses on microbial genomics, developing open-source tools for prokaryote genome analysis and visualization.

He is the co-creator of BRIG (BLAST Ring Image Generator), published in BMC Genomics, and co-hosts the MicroBinfie Podcast. GenomicX grew from a simple conviction: the tools researchers build should be usable by everyone, not just people with the same computing setup.
